[vc_row css=”.vc_custom_1496477485353{margin-bottom: 45px !important;padding-top: 35px !important;}”][vc_column][vc_custom_heading stripe_pos=”hide” text=”Cockroaches ” font_container=”tag:h2|font_size:24|text_align:left|color:%23e31d22|line_height:1.25″ use_theme_fonts=”yes”][vc_column_text]<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Cockroaches are medium-sized to large insects in the order Dictyoptera<\/li>\n
  • They are broad, flattened insects with long antennae and a prominent, shield-shaped section behind the head called a pronotum<\/li>\n
  • A cockroach has three stages during its life cycle: egg, nymph, and adult<\/li>\n
  • Cockroaches are nocturnal, hiding during the day and becoming active at night<\/li>\n
  • All cockroaches are scavengers and will survive on almost any food as well as backing glue, leather, bookbindings, bar soap<\/li>\n
  • Cockroaches are known to carry human pathogens, such as Salmonella and E. coli, which can result in human diseases, such as food poisoning or diarrhea<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      \n
    • Largest cockroach species to infest buildings<\/li>\n
    • Both the adult male and female is about 1-1\/2 to 2 inches long, reddish brown and possesses long wings that cover its abdomen<\/li>\n
    • A female typically produces 9 to 10 egg cases which are deposited carefully in a crack or crevice<\/li>\n
    • Each egg capsule contains about 12 young; a female and her offspring can produce over 800 cockroaches in one year.<\/li>\n
    • An American cockroach has a fondness for fermenting foods, e.g. bread soaked with beer<\/li>\n
    • Their foraging is confined mostly to the basement and ground floor of a building unless suitable conditions exist in higher locations<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

      • Females produce about 30 to 50 eggs at a time<\/li>\n
      • Each egg case contains about 30 young, and a female may produce a new egg case every few weeks<\/li>\n
      • The female carries the egg case until the eggs are ready to hatch<\/li>\n
      • It generally inhabits kitchens and bathrooms where they are found near plumbing fixtures, in cracks or crevices in cupboards, under drawers and kitchen sinks, and similar locations<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

          \n
        • The brownbanded cockroach is similar in size and color to the German cockroach<\/li>\n
        • It can be distinguished by the lack of brown bars on the shield in front of the wings and by the presence of two light bands across the wings and abdomen<\/li>\n
        • The adult brownbanded cockroach is about \u00bd inch long<\/li>\n
        • An adult male is golden brown and has a narrow body with its wings extending beyond the tip of its abdomen<\/li>\n
        • A female adult is dark chestnut brown, has a teardrop-shaped body, and its wings do not completely cover the abdomen<\/li>\n
        • Both sexes have distinctive horizontal yellow bands<\/li>\n
        • Brownbanded cockroaches prefer starchy food (e.g., glue on stamps and envelopes), are often found in offices and other places where paper is stored, and are more common in apartments or homes that are not air conditioned<\/li>\n
        • Females glue light brown egg cases, which are about 1\/4 inch long, to ceilings, beneath furniture, or in closets or other dark places where eggs incubate for several weeks before hatching<\/li>\n
        • Each female and her offspring are capable of producing over 600 cockroaches in one year<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n
